BHAGAT SINGH

28 September 1907 - 23 March 1931

The Legend: Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh was one of India’s most courageous and visionary revolutionaries, whose life became a symbol of resistance against British colonial rule. Born into a family with a strong history of patriotism, he grew up hearing stories of bravery, sacrifice, and the struggle for freedom. Inspired by these values from childhood, Bhagat Singh dedicated himself fully to the fight for independence, believing that true freedom required not just protest, but fearless action. As a young revolutionary, he challenged injustice in every form and became a powerful voice for the youth of India. His famous slogan “Inquilab Zindabad” became the heartbeat of the freedom movement, motivating countless people to stand up against oppression. Bhagat Singh was known not only for his bravery but also for his sharp intellect—he read extensively, wrote passionately, and dreamed of an India built on equality, justice, and unity. Even though he was executed at the young age of 23, the impact he left on the nation was far greater than his years. His sacrifice transformed him into an everlasting icon of courage, inspiring generations to fight for truth and freedom. Today, Bhagat Singh is remembered as a hero whose revolutionary spirit continues to guide and motivate India.

Biographies

  • Born on 28 September 1907 in Banga, Punjab, India.
  • Grew up in a patriotic family deeply involved in the freedom struggle.
  • Joined revolutionary groups at a very young age to oppose British rule.
  • Became a member of the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA).
  • Promoted the slogan “Inquilab Zindabad,” inspiring thousands of young Indians.
  • Involved in major revolutionary activities, including the Assembly Bombing in 1929.
  • Known for his powerful writings and strong political ideology.
  • Arrested and later executed on 23 March 1931 at the age of 23.
  • Remembered as a national hero whose sacrifice ignited India’s freedom movement.
